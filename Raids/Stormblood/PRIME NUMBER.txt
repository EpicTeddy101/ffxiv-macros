A prime number is a number that can only be divided evenly by 1 and itself, with no remainder.
Examples:
2: It's the smallest prime number. It can only be divided by 1 and 2.
3: Another prime number. It can only be divided by 1 and 3.
5: Yet another prime number. It can only be divided by 1 and 5.
Non-Examples:
4: It can be divided by 1, 2, and 4. So, it's not prime.
6: It can be divided by 1, 2, 3, and 6. So, it's not prime.
1: It can be divided by only itself. So, it's not a prime.
